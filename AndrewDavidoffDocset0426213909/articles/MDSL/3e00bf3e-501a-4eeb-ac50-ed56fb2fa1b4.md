 <b>FontVariantsSyntax</b> </td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td><div class="indent0">[values]</div></td>
 <td></td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td><div class="indent2">Inferior</div></td>
 <td>Replaces a default glyph with an inferior glyph, or it may combine glyph substitution with positioning adjustments for proper placement. Inferior forms are typically used in chemical formulas or mathematical notation.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td><div class="indent2">Normal</div></td>
 <td>Default font behavior. Font scaling and positioning is normal.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td><div class="indent2">Ordinal</div></td>
 <td>Replaces a default glyph with an ordinal glyph, or it may combine glyph substitution with positioning adjustments for proper placement. Ordinal forms are normally associated with numeric notation of an ordinal word, such as "1st" for "first."</td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td><div class="indent2">Ruby</div></td>
 <td>Replaces a default glyph with a smaller Japanese Kana glyph. This is used to clarify the meaning of Kanji, which may be unfamiliar to the reader.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td><div class="indent2">Subscript</div></td>
 <td>Replaces a default glyph with a subscript glyph.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td><div class="indent2">Superscript</div></td>
 <td>Replaces a default glyph with a superscript glyph. Superscript is commonly used for footnotes.</td>
 </tr>
